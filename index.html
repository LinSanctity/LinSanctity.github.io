Brief Introduction to My Research.

My research primarily revolves around galaxy simulations. I have used the well-known open-source code GIZMO (Hopkins et al.) on the xl cluster of ASIAA (Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics, Academia Sinica, Taiwan) and Cori at NERSC (National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center, USA) to study supermassive black hole (SMBH) and bulge growth. We discovered that major mergers can be a possible channel to explain the rapid growth of 10 9 solar mass SMBHs at high redshift. Currently, I am developing the ASURA-BRIDGE code (Fujii, Saitoh, Hirai et al.) on the XC50 cluster at CfCA (Center for Computational Astrophysics, Japan) to study the impact of Population III stars on the evolution of halos and galaxy formation. We have identified some candidate criteria for the formation of mysterious dwarf galaxies, including Ultra-faint dwarf galaxies (UFDs) and Ultra-compact dwarfs (UCDs).
